Informed Consent

Study Title

Decision-making and delegation in interactive tasks.

Please read the following information carefully

The success of our study greatly depends on you paying close attention to what you are doing. Your thoughtful participation is essential for the validity of our research.

Purpose of the Study

You are invited to participate in a research study exploring how individuals make decisions in interactive tasks. Interactive tasks are those in which multiple participants make decisions that affect each other's outcomes (so you will be paired with other human participants to participate in this experiment). The study investigates how delegation and decision-making strategies influence outcomes and beliefs in social interactions. The results will contribute to our understanding of human behavior.

Participant Requirements and Exclusion Criteria:

To participate, you must:

  • Be at least 18 years old.
  • Be based in the United States.
  • Have fluent reading and writing skills in English.
  • Have reliable internet access.
  • Not have participated in a similar study on this topic previously.
  • Successfully complete all comprehension tests and attention checks during the study.
  • Be a human participant. The use of AI, bots, or any automated tools (outside of our platform) to complete this study constitutes fraud and will result in exclusion and forfeiture of payment.

Duration and Effort:

The study will take approximately 15 minutes to complete. Please ensure you are in a quiet environment without distractions.

Remuneration:

You will receive $12 per hour, prorated based on the time taken to complete the study. Additionally, you may earn bonus payments based on the outcomes of your decisions and those of others you interact with. The bonus structure will be explained in detail during the instructions.

Study Procedure and Tasks

If you agree to participate, you will:

  • Complete a series of interactive tasks involving decision-making.
  • In some parts, you will make decisions yourself; in others, you will delegate them to an artificial agent provided on our platform.
  • After completing the comprehension test, you will be paired with other human participants. Your decisions will directly affect their experience, and theirs will affect yours.
  • Once you are paired with other participants, dropping out will disrupt the experiment for everyone involved.
  • The study consists of four parts, and you will receive instructions before each part.

You will remain anonymous throughout the study.

Voluntariness and Right to Withdraw:

Your participation is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw from the study at any time by returning your submission in Prolific.

Foreseeable Factors:

This study involves no risks beyond those encountered in everyday life. You may experience mild discomfort when making decisions that affect your and others' monetary outcomes. No strong or extraordinary emotions are expected.

Confidentiality and Anonymity:

All data collected will be kept strictly confidential and anonymous. Your identity will not be linked to your responses. The data will be stored securely and used solely for research purposes.

Scope of Data Collection and Handling

We will collect:

  • Your decisions during the tasks.
  • Basic demographic information (e.g., age, gender).
  • Timing of your decisions.
  • Responses to open-ended questions.

Handling of Collected Data:

  • Your data will be anonymized and stored for up to 5 years.
  • Only authorized researchers will have access to the data.
  • Anonymized data may be reused for future research or shared with other researchers for scientific purposes.
  • Published results will not include any identifiable information.

Right to Data Deletion:

You may request the deletion of your data at any time by contacting the researcher. However, once data is anonymized and included in analyses or publications, it cannot be retrieved or deleted.

Contact Information

If you have any questions about the study or your rights as a participant, you may contact:

  • Researcher: Dr. Ines Terrucha
  • Institution: University of Duisburg-Essen
  • Email: ines.terrucha@uni-due.de
  • Ethics Oversight: Ethics Committee of the Faculty of Computer Science of the University of Duisburg-Essen

Important to Note:

There is NO DECEPTION in this economic experiment. You will interact with other real human participants, and your decisions will affect your own monetary gains and those of the other participants you are paired with.
