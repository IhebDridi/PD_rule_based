Welcome to Part {{ part_delegation }}: Delegation

In this part of the experiment, you will delegate your decisions to an AI agent. This means that instead of making individual decisions in each round yourself, you will provide input to a supervised learning AI agent by choosing a dataset for your agent will learn from. The agent will use your input to learn your preferences and make allocation decisions on your behalf for all 10 rounds in this part.

You will first delegate your decisions in case you are the 1st mover; and then delegate your decisions in case you are the 2nd mover and the other player chose A as the 1st mover. Remember, even though you will be delegating decisions for both roles, your true role in each round will only be determined (randomly for each part) at the end of the 10 rounds.

How This Works

  1. Train your AI Agent: Before the 10 rounds begin, you will choose a table with example allocations that represent your preferences and click “Generate”. This example dataset will be used by your AI agent to learn your preferences and generate similar choices.
  2. Agent Execution: The resulting behavior of your AI agent's learning will be shown in a table on the right side of the screen, with the decisions your agent will make in each round, for each specific role. If you do not want your AI agent to proceed with the behavior you see in this table, you have two options:
    • Choose another dataset for your agent to learn based on a set of different preferences.
    • Ask your agent to learn again on the same dataset, and it will generate new behavior on the same preferences.
  3. Reshuffled co-players: Once again, you will be matched with a different co-player in each round, meaning that you might have to wait for them to also finalize their answers.
  4. Once you are satisfied with your decision: click "Confirm" to finalize your agent's settings. The AI agent will use your instructions to make decisions in all 10 rounds according to the table generated based on the dataset of your choice, for the role you are assigned to in each round. Once submitted, you will not be able to adjust the AI agent's decisions once the 10 rounds begin.
